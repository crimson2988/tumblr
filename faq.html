FAQ

Submission Questions

Do all submitted audios get posted?

Most do. Some of the reasons an audio may not be posted include, (1) poor sound quality, however this can be avoided by reading recording tips, (2) if a recording is very short, as in less than 2 minutes, (3) if there is hardly anything to hear, remember people want to hear something.

When will my audio get posted?

Normally audios are posted in the order in which they were received, however, audios with talking in them may be given a higher priority. The length of time it takes to post varies and is hard to say but usually it will be at least a couple of weeks.

How long should a recording be?

Between 3 and 20 minutes is recommended.

Does an audio have to be in a specific file format?

Not really. You can send a file via email in pretty much any audio or video file format. However, if you use the Jotform it must be in mp3 format.

How do I get on the Audios Created By list?

Submit 4 or more audios to get on the list - doesn’t have to be at the same time. You’ll also need to include a fictitious or blog name.

Is there a limit to the number of audios I can submit?

You can submit as many as you like. Be sure to include your blog name or fictitious name each time.

Is submitting an audio anonymous?

Any personal information, such as name or email address always remains private. And if you have any concerns about remaining anonymous see these recommendations.

What about unwanted comments?

If you ever see a reply posted to your audio that makes you uncomfortable in any way be sure to let me know and I will delete the comment.

Also, if you have anonymous messages enabled on your blog you are more likely to get unwanted comments. It's just a fact of the Internet that there are a few people who enjoy saying mean and stupid things while hiding behind the wall of anonymity.

All Other Questions

Can I contact someone who submitted an audio?

I suggest posting your message in the “Reply” box on the the Tumblr dashboard. People who submit audios often read replies.

What do “(L)” and “(W)” mean?

An "L" means the post uses the Tumblr audio player to play an audio that is actually hosted someplace else. However, it looks and functions the same as a regular audio post. A "W" means the preceding link is to a website and not a Tumblr blog.

Audio playback issues?

Having trouble then I suggest closing and reloading your browser, you may need to clear your browser’s cache.

And FYI for playback, you can skip forward and backward on the audios by sliding your cursor (if using a mouse) or by tapping your finger if on a phone or tablet onthe playback bar.
