Recording Tips

Sound quality and content are important so here are some tips.

How to make a recording

I recommend the app 'Voice Recorder Pro' to make recordings however there are plenty of apps available and your device likely already has one. Voice Recorder Pro has tons of functions like editing, uploading and emailing. The app makes it super easy to email your audio directly to soundsofpleasure@mail.com.

To get good sound quality

This is the most important part!

It’s best if your phone or mic is fairly close to your mouth when you’re recording, like for example on the edge of a table next to your bed or chair. What you don’t want is to have the mic directly in front of your mouth which can cause wind noise when you breathe, this can ruin the recording. Another bad sound is sucking in air through your teeth, I recommend not doing that. Usually these noises can be avoided by moving the mic to one side of your face so your breath doesn’t blow directly into the mic, try making a test recording. And don’t jostle your phone or laptop when you’re recording. This makes surprisingly loud and unwanted noises in the recording. If you have your phone or laptop on your bed be sure not to move or nudge it or have anything touch it.

You can also use a headset with a boom mic and position the mic just below and to the side of your mouth. This works well if you want to get the mic close but want your hands free. Keep in mind however the sound quality is not as good as a podcast mic and maybe not as good as your phone.

Also, keep in mind, little or no background noise is best. I recommend turning off the TV and radio although a little background music is okay as long as it’s not too loud and doesn’t compete with you.
